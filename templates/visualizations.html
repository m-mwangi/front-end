Exploring Maternal Health Risk Factors

Dive into the data to uncover insights into maternal health risks.

Age Distribution

Younger mothers are at lower risks, but risks increase significantly for older mothers.

Blood Sugar Levels

Mothers with elevated blood sugar levels (10+) face higher pregnancy risks.

Blood Pressure

This plot highlights how higher systolic blood pressure is strongly associated with elevated maternal health risks.

×

Insights:

Younger mothers (20–30) have the lowest risks, but risks escalate with age beyond 35, possibly due to complications associated with older pregnancies.

×

Insights:

  • High-risk patients have a significantly higher spread of blood sugar levels, with the median around 12 mmol/L and outliers exceeding 18 mmol/L.
  • Low-risk patients have tightly clustered blood sugar levels, typically under 7 mmol/L.
  • Mid-risk patients show moderate blood sugar levels, ranging mostly between 7 and 10 mmol/L.

×

Insights:

Each dot represents a patient, categorized by risk levels: low, mid, and high risk.

  • Patients with higher systolic blood pressure values (above 140 mmHg) are predominantly in the high-risk category.
  • Mid-risk patients mostly fall within the systolic blood pressure range of 100–140 mmHg.
  • Low-risk patients tend to have systolic blood pressure under 120 mmHg but not at the extreme lower range.
